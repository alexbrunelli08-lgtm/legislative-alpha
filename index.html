How this works

01 — Bills & appropriations

Recently-active bills from the Congress.gov API, matched to a sector by keyword. Bills are flagged when they appropriate or authorize funding, and any dollar figures visible in the title or latest action are pulled out. A 0–100 momentum score reflects the bill's stage (introduced → committee → passed a chamber → signed), its cosponsor count, and recent activity.

02 — The stocks that benefit

Each bill lists its sector's constituent companies — the names positioned to benefit if it advances. Open any bill to see the tickers and the congressional trades disclosed in exactly those stocks.

03 — Congressional disclosure

Every trade disclosed in Senate and House Periodic Transaction Reports (STOCK Act filings) over the data window — scraped from efdsearch.senate.gov and the House Clerk's e-filed PDFs. A bill is flagged when a trade in its sector was disclosed in the 45 days before it was introduced.

About this data. Bills and amendments come from the Congress.gov API; trades from Senate and House financial disclosures (House paper filings are scanned images and are skipped). "Stocks that benefit" is a sector mapping, not a prediction — sector definitions live in scripts/sectors.json. Refreshes once daily. Not investment advice.

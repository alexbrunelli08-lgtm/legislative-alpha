How this works

01 — Legislative momentum score

A 0–100 score built from real, directly observable signals: the bill's current stage (introduced → committee → passed a chamber → signed), its real cosponsor count, and whether it saw action in the last 14 days. This is a progress signal, not a predictive model — it does not estimate historical passage rates or committee-specific dynamics.

02 — Sector trading & lobbying feeds

Trades come from Senators' own Periodic Transaction Reports (STOCK Act disclosures), scraped directly from efdsearch.senate.gov. Lobbying data comes from the Senate's public Lobbying Disclosure Act filings. Both are matched to a sector via a fixed list of that sector's constituent companies — not to a specific bill.

03 — Pre-filing trade flag

A bill is flagged if a tracked trade in its sector was disclosed in the 45 days before the bill was introduced. This is a sector-level correlation, not a claim that any specific trade concerned that specific bill.

About this data. Bills and amendments come from the Congress.gov API. Congressional trades are scraped from Senate financial disclosures only — the House's disclosures are scanned PDF images and are not included. Lobbying data comes from the Senate LDA API. Data refreshes once daily via a scheduled job. Sector definitions (ETF, keyword list, constituent companies) are a fixed, hand-curated starting point — not derived from live ETF holdings data — and should be reviewed periodically.
